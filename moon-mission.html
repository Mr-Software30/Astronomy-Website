Kurdistan Voyager: First Moon Mission

A historic journey led by Kurdish astronauts, marking a new era in lunar exploration and international collaboration.

Mission Overview

On July 18, 2032, Cosmic Horizons Organization made history by launching its first crewed mission to the Moon. The Kurdistan Voyager mission showcased advanced technology, scientific ambition, and the pioneering spirit of the Kurdish people.

The Crew

  • Commander: Dr. Baran Hawrami
  • Pilot: Jiyan Shad
  • Flight Engineer: Rojin Bahjat
  • Science Officer: Soran Dilan

Mission Objectives

  • Land in Mare Serenitatis region
  • Deploy advanced scientific instruments
  • Collect and return lunar samples
  • Test new life-support and habitat systems
  • Broadcast live educational sessions

Highlights & Achievements

  • First successful Kurdish-piloted lunar landing
  • Discovery of undetected water ice deposits
  • Deployment of the "Newroz Beacon" relay
  • 48-hour lunar surface EVA with full scientific payload return
  • Global audience of 500 million for the landing broadcast

The Spaceship: Kurdistan Voyager

Designed for deep space travel, Kurdistan Voyager featured state-of-the-art navigation, radiation shielding, and modular living quarters. Its name honors the resilience and aspirations of the Kurdish people.

Legacy

The mission set new standards for international space exploration and inspired a new generation of scientists and explorers. The crew's achievements are celebrated annually on "Voyager Day."
